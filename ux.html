Cayuga Health Partners (CHP) Community-based Organizations (CBO) Web Interface

Summer 2020

Description: Based on the social determinants of health, I worked with physicians at Cayuga Medical Center, students at Cornell University Masters of Public Health, members of Cornell Health Equity, and the IT department of CHP to design a search engine for community-based organizations (CBO) in Tompkins County. This website would allow physicians to search through these CBOs and refer their patients to each of them, as well as patients to search through these organizations on their own.

Problem

The only CBO search engine available for physicians and patients was the 2-1-1 page for Tompkins County. The existing web page is difficult to navigate through and outdated. There is no webpage that consolidates all the CBOs.

Goal

To design a search engine that is minimalistic and patient-centered. Contain appropriate filters for both the physicians and patients to use.

Process

I researched the different sectors of social determinants of health and applied the most relevant ones to Tompkins County. I interviewed physicians about how they would like to use the site to refer their patients. I worked with the IT department to create a cohesive site.

Hinder: Find Your Perfect Housing Match!

Fall 2019

Description: Took Human Computer Interaction (HCI) and participated in a semester-long project with three other group members.

Persona: Matt

Based on interviews with students who are struggling to find housing on Cornell campus, we developed a persona that encompasses the ideal user to our product. End goals for Matt: Understand available housing options, Settle on a living situation that maximizes productivity & has a beneficial impact on his wellbeing.

Problem

Due to the lack of resources regarding off-campus housing options, many Cornell students have had very stressful and unpleasant experiences with the housing search process. This has resulted in dissatisfied living situations and unnecessary burdens on students.

Goal

The goal is to improve the housing situation for Cornell students by providing resources for students who do not have housing, who cannot afford housing on campus, and who did poorly in the housing lottery.
